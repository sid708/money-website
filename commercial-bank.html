Commercial Bank

Role and importance in the economy

Introduction

Commercial banks are financial institutions that deal directly with the public. They play a vital role in economic development by mobilizing savings and providing credit.

Functions of Commercial Banks

Commercial banks accept deposits, provide loans, facilitate payments, and offer financial services such as ATM access and online banking.

Credit Creation

Banks create credit by lending a portion of deposited money. This process increases the money supply and supports economic activity.

Importance

Commercial banks support businesses, encourage savings, and help individuals meet financial needs.

Conclusion

By connecting savers and borrowers, commercial banks form the backbone of modern financial systems.
