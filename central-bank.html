Central Bank

Controller of a nation’s monetary system

Introduction

The central bank is the highest monetary authority of a country. In India, this role is performed by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).

Functions

The central bank issues currency, controls money supply, acts as banker to the government, and regulates commercial banks.

Monetary Policy

Through interest rates and open market operations, the central bank controls inflation and economic stability.

Lender of Last Resort

The central bank provides emergency funds to banks during financial crises.

Conclusion

The central bank ensures financial stability and confidence in the monetary system.
