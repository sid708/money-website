Types of Money

Different forms money takes in an economy

Introduction

Money exists in various forms depending on how it is used and accepted. Over time, societies have developed different types of money to meet changing economic needs.

Commodity Money

Commodity money has intrinsic value. Examples include gold, silver, salt, and cattle. These items were valuable even without being used as money.

Fiat Money

Fiat money has no intrinsic value and derives its value from government authority. Paper currency and coins used today are examples of fiat money.

Credit Money

Credit money includes cheques, credit cards, and bank deposits. It represents a promise to pay rather than physical cash.

Digital Money

Digital money exists electronically and is used through online banking, mobile wallets, and payment apps. It has become increasingly popular due to convenience.

Conclusion

Each type of money plays an important role in modern economies. Together, they support smooth financial transactions and economic stability.
