Functions of Money

How money supports economic activity

Introduction

Money performs several important functions that make economic transactions smooth and efficient. Without these functions, modern economies would struggle to operate effectively.

Medium of Exchange

The primary function of money is to act as a medium of exchange. It eliminates the problems of barter by allowing people to buy and sell goods easily. Money is universally accepted, which makes trade faster and more efficient.

Unit of Account

Money provides a common measure of value. Prices of goods and services are expressed in monetary terms, making it easier to compare values and make economic decisions.

Store of Value

Money allows people to save purchasing power for future use. Unlike perishable goods, money can be stored without losing value immediately, although inflation can reduce its real value.

Standard of Deferred Payments

Money is used to settle future payments such as loans, salaries, and rent. This function supports credit systems and long-term contracts.

Conclusion

These functions make money essential to both individuals and businesses. By simplifying trade and supporting savings and investment, money strengthens economic stability.
